## 🎯 Pricing Philosophy

**Core Principles:**
1. **Value-based pricing** - Price based on value delivered, not cost to build
2. **Psychological pricing** - Use £9, £19, £49 (not £10, £20, £50)
3. **Anchor pricing** - Show higher price first to make actual price seem low
4. **Tiered options** - Give 3 choices (most people pick middle)

## 💡 Psychological Pricing Tactics

### 1. The Rule of 9
- ❌ £10.00
- ✅ £9.99 (feels significantly cheaper)

### 2. Price Anchoring
- Show "Regular price: £29" crossed out
- Show "Your price: £9"
- Makes £9 feel like a steal

### 3. Decoy Pricing
```
Basic: £9 (limited features)
Pro: £19 (most features) ← Target
Enterprise: £49 (features most don't need)
```
Most people pick the middle option.

### 4. Loss Aversion
- "Don't risk £30k fines" (fear of loss)
- Works better than "Save money"

### 5. Social Proof Pricing
- "Join 1,000+ UK businesses"
- "Most popular choice"

## 🎯 Price Increase Strategy

### When to Increase Prices

**Signals:**
- Conversion rate > 5% (too low)
- Customers saying "this is too cheap"
- Competitors charging 2-3x more
- Product significantly improved

**How to Increase:**
1. Announce 30 days in advance
2. Grandfather existing customers
3. Offer "lock in current price" for 48 hours
4. Add new features to justify increase

**Example:**
```
⚠️ Price Increase Announcement

On April 1, GDPR Cookie Auditor will increase from £9 to £19.

Why? We've added [new features].

Lock in the £9 price forever:
[Buy Now]

Existing customers: Your price stays at £9.
```

## 🎁 Discount Strategy

### When to Offer Discounts

**Good Reasons:**
- Product Hunt launch (40% off for 48 hours)
- Email subscribers (exclusive 20% off)
- First 100 customers (early bird pricing)
- Bundle purchases (buy 2, get 20% off)

**Bad Reasons:**
- Desperation (no sales)
- Competitor price matching
- No clear strategy

### Discount Codes

**GDPR Cookie Auditor:**
